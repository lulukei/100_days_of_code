100 days of code

Presented on this website are visual projects based on the coding skills I aquired during the 100-day code challenge (November 29th-March 9th). During that time, I learned the basics of p5.js, HTML & CSS.

Why starting to code?

I think it is a mix of professional pragmatism and me wanting to express myself artistically.

Was it hard?

Yes and no. It is really true that you just need 21 days to make something a habit. After a 3 weeks, no doing my daily coding felt like not brushing my teeth.
That being said, a lot of motivation comes from the group dynamics. The challenge started with my co-workers ot Mimi. After 10 days, we were 2 people left out of 8. The last colleague "alive", Kevin, took a break arund Christmas time and I wasn't sure whether he vould come back. My motivation seriouslyflickered at that moment. I harrassed him day and night so he would continue. Luckily he resumed the challenge quickly.

A piece of advice to those who would like to start this challenge?

Genius is one percent inspiration and 99% perspiration (Thomas Edison)
So get to work!
